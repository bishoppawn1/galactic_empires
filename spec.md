# Galactic Empires — Prototype Specification

## Product goal

Galactic Empires is a real-time browser strategy game about expanding from one homeworld into a contested star system. Most play happens on the space map; planets open focused economy, production, and ground-battle views.

## Core loop

1. A new campaign opens on a setup screen. The player chooses the Human Coalition, Brood, Aegis Directorate, or Iron Covenant; a Compact (7 worlds), Standard (11 worlds), Expansive (15 worlds), or Colossal (21 worlds) galaxy; and Cadet, Commander, or Admiral enemy difficulty. Difficulty changes AI construction cadence, attack frequency, combat strength, and escort size. The campaign can instead launch as a two-to-four-empire competitive match from a lobby with a six-character code.
2. The player and hostile AI each begin on exactly one colonized planet with no ground units or ships, so every empire must produce its first forces. Mineral economies begin with Metal, Crystal, and Gold plus one of each mine, a Ground Factory, and a Space Yard. The Brood instead begins with Biomass, a Ground Factory, and a Space Yard because its worlds grow resources without mines. Every unclaimed planet begins with a deterministic garrison of one or two neutral ground units.
3. Mines add Metal, Crystal, and Gold to a shared imperial stockpile indefinitely. Base collection runs at four times the prototype's original rate so the opening economy reaches useful decisions quickly. The Brood gains four Biomass per second from every controlled planet and recovers additional Biomass from destroyed forces in ground or orbital battles in which it participates. The Iron Covenant reclaims 25 percent of every destroyed unit's Metal value from battles in which it participates, with surviving Salvage Frigates increasing orbital recovery by 50 percent. Planets and resources never deplete.
4. The player spends resources on additional buildings, units, ships, and research. Ground Factories, Advanced Ground Factories, Space Yards, and Advanced Space Yards have no per-planet construction limit; all other structures retain their planetary maxima. The hostile empire runs its own independent economy under the same construction rules. While neutral worlds remain, its transport missions follow a two-expansions-to-one-invasion cadence, choose the nearest reachable target of the preferred type, and fall back to the other mission type when necessary. Enemy colonies maintain two to four troop carriers by difficulty and commit multiple loaded transports to invasions. Combat fleets operate independently of transports: rear colonies retain a small difficulty-scaled defensive reserve and promptly deploy every surplus warship to threatened colonies, active invasion targets, or hostile systems.
5. Every planet is surrounded by a 780-pixel-radius gravity well on a 12800 × 8800 tactical canvas. Ship centers are capped at a 700-pixel radius and crowded fleets fill bounded concentric orbit rings. Spawn slots, idle positions, and formation destinations keep ship centers at least 40 pixels apart: frigate artwork deliberately overlaps, but each hull is visibly offset so the fleet count can be estimated instead of disappearing into one stack. Maneuvering ships still cross paths continuously without teleporting. Docked fleets form the same compact stagger above the central docking perimeter while remaining mechanically docked. The well is click-through for maneuver orders, while the planet center remains a docking target. Ships have persistent orbit positions and can be drag-box selected, Shift-selected, and moved as formations within the well. Formation commands support up to 512 ships, so selections above 70 move, dock, and travel together without an obsolete multiplayer-validation cutoff. Selecting one or more player ships opens a compact fleet HUD with one ship glyph per unit and separate, unlabeled hull and shield bars directly beneath every glyph. Clicking a hostile ship, including one drawn in the large-fleet canvas layer or making a landing approach, opens a hostile inspection HUD with separate hull and shield bars but grants no movement authority. Left-click is reserved for selection and inspection; right-clicking an open point inside the fleet's current gravity well issues a maneuver order. Clicking empty space outside the selected fleet's current well clears ship selection. The current galaxy zoom persists while the commander visits Research or follows a Ground Battle. Orbital movement and landing approaches are deliberately slow and occur visibly over time rather than teleporting to the destination.
6. Holding W, A, S, or D continuously pans the galaxy camera up, left, down, or right, including diagonal movement when two keys are held. Scrollbars are hidden so navigation is handled directly on the map. Dedicated zoom-out, zoom-in, 1:1, and Show Whole Map controls are always visible; zoom changes only through these explicit controls, mouse-wheel input never changes the map scale, and Show Whole Map calculates a fit from the current viewport within the 2% to 150% range. Claimed planets use redundant ownership cues: their sphere tint, orbit ring, and badge immediately adopt the current owner's color, using cyan and YOU for the local commander with magenta, gold, and violet identities for rival empires plus a counted faction legend. Unscouted systems—including rival homeworlds—appear neutral and reveal no controller, structures, garrison, battles, or orbiting ships until a friendly ship enters the gravity well. Each empire keeps its own last-known planetary reconnaissance after leaving, but later ownership, structure, and ground-force changes remain hidden until the next visit; enemy ships never remain in stale reports. Live enemy ships are visible only in player-controlled systems, systems containing a player ship, or while traveling toward one of those systems. Hidden-system strategic messages and conflict counts are also suppressed. Planet centers use 190-pixel interaction targets with 86-pixel planet spheres, orbiting ships use 32-pixel targets, and transports display a high-contrast 12-pixel cargo badge with a distinct full-hold state.
7. Nearby gravity wells are joined by long, thin phase lanes. Selecting a fleet reveals visible JUMP gates just beyond its gravity-well line on every outbound lane. Right-clicking a connected star system or its gate sends the fleet across that lane; right-clicking a system two or more lanes away automatically plots the shortest available route across the network. Ships first move slowly from their current orbit to the outbound system border, hold there for a two-second gate charge, then travel through the phase tunnel faster than the previous lane speed. Transit ships appear as unlabeled moving hulls on the galaxy map. Their markers use the exact simulated coordinates without list-size offsets, so large departing fleets follow straight, stable paths instead of being displaced into an artificial spiral or column. During system exit and gate charging, ships remain targetable and exchange orbital fire; player ships remain selectable and a local maneuver order cancels the jump at their exact current position. Entering the phase tunnel commits the jump and removes the ships from local combat. Route phase and arrival timing remain available in the destination panel. Multi-lane routes repeat the slow cross-system exit and gate charge at every intermediate waypoint. At the final destination, player ships fill a separated lane-facing entry formation from the arrival edge inward, keeping even large fleets beside the phase lane they used instead of wrapping around the planet. They remain under immediate control, while AI combat ships automatically advance into open inner-orbit positions and close on hostile ships or installations.
8. Moving a Transport onto the planet center begins a visible embarkation approach and automatically loads up to four available squads only after the ship reaches the planet. Embarking transports remain targetable and are prioritized by hostile orbital forces; if one is destroyed before loading completes, its waiting ground squads remain on the planet. A loaded Transport emerging from a phase lane must cross the gravity well on a visible landing approach before unloading at the planet. Player landing craft remain selectable throughout the approach. After unloading, the Transport stays docked at the planet center instead of snapping back into orbit. Defending ships and orbital weapons prioritize landing craft during this interception window; destroying the transport also destroys its embarked army. Landing on an unclaimed planet starts a real-time battle against any neutral garrison; the planet is colonized only after that resistance is defeated. Landing on an enemy planet follows the same ground-battle rules. Additional transports landing while that battle is active reinforce the matching side, so simultaneous armies join one engagement without being overwritten.
9. Victory adds the planet to the empire. Defeat destroys the invading force.

## Resources

- Metal, Crystal, and Gold are global stockpiles for the Human Coalition, Aegis Directorate, and Iron Covenant.
- The Brood replaces all three mineral stockpiles and costs with Biomass. It cannot construct mineral mines.
- Every Brood-controlled planet naturally produces 4 Biomass per second. Quantum Extraction increases that planetary output by 25 percent.
- After a battle casualty, every participating Brood empire recovers 35 percent of the destroyed units' converted Biomass value. Enemy and friendly ground units, friendly or hostile ships, and dead embarked cargo all count.
- After a battle casualty, every participating Iron Covenant empire reclaims 25 percent of the destroyed units' Metal cost. Enemy and friendly machines, ships, and embarked cargo count; a surviving Salvage Frigate raises orbital recovery by 50 percent.
- Resources on every planet are unlimited and never run dry.
- Each planet has a different maximum number of Metal Mines, Crystal Extractors, and Gold Mines, plus limits for its defensive and support structures. Both Ground Factory types and both Space Yard types are unlimited.
- Metal Mine costs Crystal + Gold.
- Crystal Extractor costs Metal + Gold.
- Gold Mine costs Metal + Crystal.
- Mineral empires may exchange any whole amount of at least 3 standard resources for either of the others at a fixed 3:1 rate. The player sets the amount to spend and receives one third of it, including fractional output when necessary. Trades require the full input amount and never involve Biomass.
- Buildings have no upgrades. Capped structures show `built / maximum`; Ground Factories and all three Space Yard classes show `built / ∞` and can always receive another construction order when their resource and research requirements are met. Each standard Ground Factory contributes 1× ground-production capacity, while each Advanced Ground Factory contributes 2.5×; these capacities add together before research bonuses are applied.

## Playable factions

- The Human Coalition is the balanced combined-arms baseline with broad technology and no severe weakness.
- The Brood is the growth and swarm faction. It uses a complete, exclusive living roster alongside its Biomass economy: Brood players and AI cannot produce Coalition units, and other civilizations cannot produce Brood organisms.
- The Aegis Directorate is the armored-formation faction. Its smaller, expensive roster uses strong hulls and shields, slow movement and construction, bonus shield regeneration, and overlapping defensive systems that reward deliberate positioning.
- Aegis ground production consists of Warden Cohorts, Bastion Tanks, Rampart Artillery, Paladin Guards, and Fortress Walkers. Its fleet consists of Bastion Landers, Shield Monitors, Lance Frigates, Sentinel Flak Frigates, Ward Cruisers, Citadel Mega-Carriers, and the unique Sovereign Titan, all with dedicated white, gold, and cyan artwork.
- The Iron Covenant is the mechanical-attrition faction. Its low-shield ground machines regenerate 0.5 hull per second in battle, Repair Drones and Iron Bulwarks restore 1 additional hull per second to nearby allies, and every ground or orbital casualty can be reclaimed as Metal. Sustained focus fire remains reliable counterplay because repairs slow damage instead of erasing it.
- Covenant ground production consists of Iron Cohorts, Bastion Striders, Furnace Artillery, Repair Drones, and Juggernaut Engines, with Iron Bulwarks deployed by Ground Defenses. Its fleet consists of Assembly Arks, Salvage Frigates, Chain Frigates, Interdictor Flak Frigates, Foundry Cruisers, Fabricator Mega-Carriers, Ironclad Battleships, and the unique Dreadforge Titan, all with dedicated gunmetal, blue-light, and furnace-orange artwork.
- Faction identity is stored separately from map ownership, allowing any multiplayer command seat to carry its own civilization, economy, and future roster through perspective translation.

## Multiplayer

- Multiplayer is a two-to-four-player free-for-all. The host controls Empire 1 and each joining commander receives a separate Empire 2, 3, or 4 rather than sharing units, colonies, resources, production, research, or targeting orders.
- Starting multiplayer opens a lobby with a case-insensitive six-character code. Join Game appears immediately below Start Multiplayer and accepts that code.
- The lobby holds up to four empire slots. Every joining commander brings the faction selected on their own campaign setup screen, and the full roster displays each empire's civilization before launch. Only the host can start, and launch remains disabled until at least one rival human or AI empire has joined. The host can add or remove AI empire slots before launch; a disconnected human empire transfers to AI control so the remaining match can continue.
- Every empire begins on its own homeworld with its commander's selected faction, starting economy, and structures; guest players are not restricted to the Human Coalition, and empires using the same faction receive symmetric starts. Matches with three or four empires automatically use the Colossal 21-world frontier. Human-only slots disable strategic AI; configured AI slots use the chosen difficulty.
- The host owns the deterministic simulation clock and validates every empire's commands. Guests send ground and Space Yard production, construction, research, fleet, maneuver, and targeting orders to the host, then each receives the authoritative state translated into their own empire's perspective.
- Browser peers connect through WebRTC. Authoritative snapshots use PeerJS binary serialization so large late-game states are automatically chunked, and full-state synchronization is capped at four updates per second to prevent channel-buffer overload. Player commands remain immediate. The lobby exists only while the host remains online; no account or cloud save is required.
- Multiplayer signaling has a finite connection timeout, authoritative snapshots are validated before installation, and the host can continue after an individual guest disconnects. The PeerJS multiplayer client ships with the initial application bundle so Join Game never depends on an expired lazy-loaded chunk; any future stale deployment-chunk event triggers one guarded refresh to the current version.

## Buildings

- Metal Mine, Crystal Extractor, Gold Mine
- Ground Factory and Advanced Ground Factory
- Space Yard, Advanced Space Yard, and Experimental Space Yard
- Ground Defenses, Anti-Space Battery, Space Defenses
- Research Lab

Advanced factories are gated by Advanced Industry. The Experimental Space Yard is gated by Capital Ship Doctrine and functions as the Tier 3 yard.
Defensive structures are not active immediately: Ground Defenses require 8 seconds, Anti-Space Batteries require 10 seconds, and Orbital Defenses require 12 seconds to construct. Their construction cards show the live completion countdown. When a defensive structure is destroyed, that planet cannot begin another structure of the same type for 10 seconds; the remaining rebuild lock is visible in construction and is enforced equally for human and AI empires.
Every Space Defense is represented by a distinct armed orbital platform inside its planet's gravity well. Platforms use the owning faction's color and have full-well weapon coverage, automatically damaging hostile ships even at the farthest opposite edge of the 780-pixel gravity well so long-range frigates cannot kite them.
Anti-Space Batteries are protected surface emplacements. They fire on hostile ships from the planet but cannot be selected, approached as an installation target, damaged, or destroyed by orbital ship weapons; only true Orbital Defense Platforms are destructible targets in space combat.
Every Ground Defense deploys one stationary, long-range Defense Turret whenever its planet is invaded, even if no mobile ground troops are present. Brood defenses instead grow a tougher, longer-ranged Spine Tower, while Covenant defenses deploy a self-maintaining Iron Bulwark. All are visible and targetable on the ground battlefield. A destroyed emplacement removes its corresponding Ground Defense building; surviving installations remain ready for later invasions.

Ground-unit production cards, deployed-force rows, and battlefield labels use the unit name without appending its weapon name. Compact health, shield, range, and special-ability information remains separate from that identity; ship readouts may continue to name their weapons.

## Units and production

The Ground Factory produces Infantry, Anti-Vehicle Infantry, Light Recon Vehicles, Light Tanks, and Light Artillery. Advanced Ground Factories produce shielded Shock Troopers after Ground Warfare and add Railgun Tanks, Plasma Tanks, and long-range Siege Walkers after Heavy Armor. Spaceships use three strict production tiers: Tier 1 frigates and transports require a standard Space Yard; Tier 2 cruisers require an Advanced Space Yard; and Tier 3 super-capitals require an Experimental Space Yard. A higher-tier yard cannot substitute for a lower-tier yard. The Coalition's Tier 1 roster is the Transport, Escort Frigate, Missile Frigate, and Weapons-Calibration-gated Flak Frigate. Orbital Engineering unlocks its Tier 2 Light Cruiser and Phase Cruiser. Tier 3 contains the eight-squad Atlas Mega Carrier, Battlecruiser, and unique Titan Dreadnought. The carrier deploys eight Falcon Fighters that circle and attack a hostile ship, suffer combat attrition, and rebuild one replacement every 18 seconds. Flak Frigates screen one hostile fighter wing at a time with compact rapid-tracking cannons. Every ship class uses its own top-down hull artwork in production, force lists, orbit, selection status, and phase transit; map scale increases visibly from transports through frigates, cruisers, carriers, and dreadnoughts. Ship art has no circular marker background. Player-controlled ships use a square control frame that brightens when selected, while hostile ships remain unframed. Ships turn to face their current maneuver or route direction and retain their last heading when stationary. Every ground-force class, including deployed Defense Turrets, uses distinct top-down artwork in production, force lists, and tactical battles.

The Brood replaces that production roster rather than reskinning its names. Basic ground organisms are Broodling Packs, Acid Spitters, Skitterers, Carapace Beasts, and Spore Lobbers. Ground Warfare unlocks Synapse Guards; Heavy Armor unlocks Crusher Beasts, Acid Behemoths, and Siege Crawlers. Its Tier 1 living fleet contains five-organism Spore Arks, Claw Frigates, long-range Needle Frigates, and Weapons-Calibration-gated Sporeguard Frigates. Orbital Engineering unlocks its Tier 2 Hive Cruiser and Void Stalker Cruiser. Tier 3 contains the Brood Mega-Carrier, Leviathan, and unique World Eater Titan. Every Brood organism has a visible mechanical identity: packs gain swarm damage, acid organisms and Spine Towers corrode targets, Skitterers evade damage, Carapace Beasts reflect it, bombardment organisms deal splash damage, Synapse Guards and Hive Cruisers strengthen nearby allies, Crushers break fortifications, Spore Arks regenerate anywhere, Claw Frigates hunt loaded transports, Needle Frigates pierce shields, Sporeguards fire fighter-tracking spores, Void Stalkers phase through damage, Brood Mega-Carriers split ten Ripper Spawn across targets and regrow one every 12 seconds, Leviathans heal by devouring enemies, and World Eaters inflict double damage on orbital platforms. Every Brood ground organism, Spine Tower, and spaceship uses dedicated organic artwork with its own silhouette throughout production, force lists, ground combat, orbit, and phase transit; Brood units never reuse color-filtered Coalition art.
Older Brood campaign saves automatically convert already deployed, embarked, queued, in-transit, and battling Coalition units into their equivalent living organisms while preserving hull and shield damage percentages.

The Aegis Ground Factory produces Warden Cohorts, Bastion Tanks, and Rampart Artillery. Advanced factories add Paladin Guards after Ground Warfare and Fortress Walkers after Heavy Armor. Every class changes formation tactics: shielded Wardens reduce nearby damage, firing Bastion Tanks anchor for extra protection, Ramparts deal bonus damage to moving targets, Paladins intercept damage aimed at nearby allies, and Fortress Walkers strike clustered enemies with a shockwave. Aegis Tier 1 ships are the five-squad Bastion Lander, Shield Monitor, Lance Frigate, and Weapons-Calibration-gated Sentinel Flak Frigate. The Ward Cruiser is Tier 2. Its Tier 3 ships are the twelve-squad Citadel Mega-Carrier and unique Sovereign Titan. Landers harden during landing and embark approaches, Shield Monitors visibly project damage reduction and extra shield regeneration to nearby ships, Lance Frigates gain damage with firing distance, Sentinel frigates track hostile fighters with precise flak emitters, Ward Cruisers intercept nearby ship targets, Citadel Mega-Carriers repair allied hulls while deploying eight armored Sentinel Interceptors that rebuild every 22 seconds, and Sovereign barrages splash across clustered fleets. Production commands and AI planning use the active civilization roster, and older Aegis saves convert temporary Coalition units into the matching Directorate classes.

The Iron Covenant Ground Factory produces Iron Cohorts, Bastion Striders, and Furnace Artillery. Ground Warfare unlocks advanced Repair Drones, while Heavy Armor unlocks Juggernaut Engines. Cohorts gain modular cross-chassis targeting, Striders begin behind ablative armor, Furnace rounds break shields, Repair Drones maintain nearby machines, and Juggernaut shells damage clustered targets. Covenant Tier 1 ships are the six-squad Assembly Ark, Salvage Frigate, Chain Frigate, and Weapons-Calibration-gated Interdictor Flak Frigate. The Foundry Cruiser is Tier 2. Its Tier 3 roster contains the Fabricator Mega-Carrier, Ironclad Battleship, and unique Dreadforge Titan. Assembly Arks and Foundries repair nearby ships, Salvage Frigates improve orbital recovery, Chain Frigates finish damaged hulls, Interdictors predict fighter flightpaths, Fabricators split eight manufactured fighters across targets and print one replacement every 14 seconds, Ironclads reduce incoming damage, and Dreadforges dismantle orbital platforms. Production commands and AI planning use this exclusive roster, and older Covenant saves convert temporary Coalition units and queues into matching machines.

Advanced ground units require both their listed research project and an Advanced Ground Factory. Every spaceship requires its listed research and the exact Space Yard class for its tier.

Production uses real-time queues. Every standard or advanced Ground Factory on a planet adds another 1× of ground-production speed, immediately accelerating the active queue. Ground-unit production estimates and queue countdowns show tenths of a second whenever the effective time is fractional instead of rounding to a whole second. Completed ground units deploy to the planet; completed ships deploy into distinct orbital slots instead of stacking at the planet center.

Every standard, advanced, or experimental Space Yard appears as its own selectable station in the planet's gravity well and owns an independent production queue. Normal hull orders automatically rotate across all yards compatible with that exact tier: the first order goes to Yard 1, the second to Yard 2, and so on before wrapping back to the shortest queue. Constructing another Space Yard immediately balances compatible waiting hulls across the expanded network while leaving each existing yard's active hull and its progress in place. Clicking a yard inspects it without disabling automatic distribution. Shift-clicking two or more yards creates an explicit group; in that mode, one separately paid ship is added to every grouped yard, and every selected yard must match the hull tier. Legacy planet-level ship queues are migrated into compatible yard queues when a local save loads. Each civilization may have only one Titan active across its orbital forces, production queues, and fleets; another can be commissioned only after that Titan is destroyed.

## Enemy AI

- The hostile empire has a separate Metal, Crystal, and Gold stockpile and earns unlimited income from mines on enemy worlds.
- Starting from one world and no units, enemy governors spend the same starting stockpile on mines, factories, research labs, advanced factories, defensive bases, and production queues under the normal costs, research gates, and planet limits.
- Enemy factories maintain ground and ship queues, unlock advanced technology over time, and produce stronger late-game forces when they can afford them.
- Once a carrier group and landing force are ready, the AI selects a reachable player colony, sends two to four loaded transports plus every escort above its local reserve over the phase-lane network, automatically lands their troops together, and can capture that planet through the same real-time ground-combat rules.
- During strategic and fleet-operation cycles, each eligible rear colony forms a transport-independent combat fleet from every warship above its defensive reserve. These fleets reinforce friendly systems under orbital attack first, coordinate with active invasions next, and otherwise strike reachable hostile colonies. Cadet reserves are larger and more conservative, while Admiral fleets launch earlier and retain only a minimal reserve.
- Campaign difficulty controls enemy construction cadence, attack frequency, escort size, and combat strength rather than granting extra starting worlds or units.

## Combat and recovery

- Ground battles run in real time on a scrollable 2600 × 1600 spatial planetary battlefield. The oversized tactical zone provides wide deployment areas and room for formations to close over distance. The player opens a contested planet from the galaxy map and exits with the white back arrow in the upper-left.
- Every ground unit has a battlefield position, movement speed, HP, shields, and its own named weapon with a distinct range, damage, projectile count, reload interval, and visual effect. Every mobile troop and stationary emplacement also has a circular personal hitbox; deployment, pursuit, and manual formation movement keep unit centers at least 104 battlefield pixels apart so ground units never stack directly beside one another. The player can drag a selection box around friendly mobile troops, click individual troops, or Shift-add troops to a formation, then right-click the battlefield to send the whole group to a destination. Ordered units hold their assigned positions instead of resuming an automatic advance.
- Ground units automatically fire deterministic weapon salvos at any hostile already inside range, including while following a manual movement order. When a unit takes fire from outside its own range, it abandons its current waypoint, pursues the strongest attacker, and returns fire once that attacker is in range; a new manual order can cancel that pursuit. Weapon ranges are visible around units, selected troops and their destination lines are highlighted, and physically small, glow-backed weapon-specific projectile artwork connects attackers to targets without obscuring the battlefield. The number of visible bolts matches each weapon's projectile count, while beam weapons stretch between attacker and target. The player can still select an enemy to focus allied targeting.
- Ships in orbit provide orbital bombardment to their faction's ground combatants only while no opposing ships remain in the system. Each supporting ship deals one damage per second in total, divided evenly among opposing ground units and absorbed by shields before hull.
- Space encounters resolve in real time on the galaxy map when hostile ships share an orbit, but an attacker fires only while its target is inside that hull's weapon range. Every ship has a named, discrete weapon profile and reload cadence: Escort Frigates maintain frequent three-emitter laser fire, Missile Frigates launch one powerful missile only after a long reload, carriers launch persistent fighter wings that circle and fire on their targets, and cruisers and capital ships use their own pulse, kinetic, railgun, or siege batteries. Carrier damage scales with the surviving fighter count. Fighter wings fly smooth, broad attack rings outside the target hull, with a visible orbit guide, engine glow, and attack traces. Deployed fighters are attackable combat assets with 40 hit points each; anti-fighter damage accumulates against the active wing, destroys individual fighters, and immediately reduces its damage output. Surviving wings still suffer deterministic combat attrition, while their parent hull slowly rebuilds replacements in orbit or transit. Each civilization has a compact flak frigate that prioritizes one hostile fighter wing in range, directs visible flak fire at the deployed craft, and deals 50% bonus damage to fighters; once no fighter target remains it resumes normal ship or orbital-platform targeting. Ships do not automatically close distance, so positioning and gravity-well maneuver orders determine which weapons can engage; selected ships show their range ring. Missile Frigates outrange Escort Frigates, while capital hulls and installations have their own finite ranges. Visible weapon fire uses distinct transparent artwork for laser, missile, pulse, kinetic, artillery, railgun, plasma, siege, and drone effects. Orbital projectile sprites use a prominent, high-contrast scale plus luminous cores, and sustained installation fire keeps cycling instead of disappearing after its first animation. Projectiles animate from attacker to target, lasers and siege weapons appear as continuous, glow-backed beams, and a Missile Frigate shows only one missile during its infrequent firing window. Those windows come from the same targeting and reload calculation as combat damage. Orbital Defense Platforms have persistent hull and shields, receive in-range return fire, and are removed when destroyed; protected surface Anti-Space Batteries continue firing without becoming orbital targets. When player ships are present, an enemy orbital platform can be clicked to mark it as the priority target.
- Large fleets remain fully simulated while their hull presentation uses a bounded rendering budget. Non-interactive hostile orbit and transit hulls are drawn together on a viewport-sized canvas instead of creating one filtered DOM layer per ship. Player-controlled ships remain individually mounted during every orbit, departure, gate-charge, and committed tunnel phase, so selection state and canvas bookkeeping never control their visibility. The hostile-ship canvas follows every camera recenter, pan, resize, and zoom even when React development checks cancel an earlier scheduled measurement. Every active orbital salvo, carrier sortie, and individual projectile remains visible regardless of battle size; their physically small sprites keep the battlefield readable. Peaceful single-faction systems bypass hostile-target searches, and local campaign autosaves are serialized at most once per second rather than on every simulation frame.
- After a ground battle, surviving ground units restore all HP and shields.
- Every ship continuously restores 5 shield points per second in friendly, hostile, or neutral orbit and throughout phase transit. Ships restore hull HP only while orbiting a friendly planet.
- Aegis ships restore an additional 3 shield points per second in orbit and phase transit. Aegis ground units restore 1.5 shield points per second during active battles.
- Orbital Defense platforms continuously regenerate 16 shield points and 2 hull points per second, capped at their normal maximums. Platform shields therefore recover eight times faster than platform hull.
- Ship salvo damage, Orbital Defenses, and Anti-Space Batteries receive the 4× orbital-combat multiplier. A properly positioned Escort Frigate can destroy a full-health Transport during its complete landing approach despite normal shield regeneration.

## Research

Research is an empire-level top navigation tab rather than a planet-panel section. It requires a Research Lab and is purchased with the empire's faction resource. The research screen is a connected, horizontally branching technology lattice with visible prerequisite lines and five named doctrine lanes rather than a set of tier rows. Its palette, doctrine names, descriptions, and unlock language specialize for the Human Coalition, Brood, Aegis Directorate, or Iron Covenant.

The lattice contains 18 projects. Its shared foundation branches into ground warfare, fleet logistics, orbital engineering, resource science, and production. Ground doctrine leads to Heavy Armor and Planetary Fortifications; fleet doctrine leads to Carrier Operations and Phase Mastery; orbital doctrine leads to Shield Harmonics and through Capital Ship Doctrine to Weapons Calibration and Titan Engineering; resource science leads to Deep-Core Extraction; and production leads to Rapid Fabrication. Rapid Fabrication accelerates all unit production by 25 percent, Planetary Fortifications increases ground and orbital defense durability by 25 percent, Phase Mastery shortens phase-lane travel by 25 percent, Shield Harmonics increases ship shield regeneration by 50 percent, and Weapons Calibration increases ship and orbital weapon damage by 15 percent. Quantum Extraction permanently increases mineral mine income or Brood planetary Biomass income by 25 percent, while Deep-Core Extraction increases the total bonus to 50 percent. Every node visibly lists what it unlocks, and larger late-game construction must be researched first.

Three end-branch capstones can be researched without a level limit. Industrial Iteration adds another 5 percent unit-production speed per level, Resource Synthesis adds another 5 percent resource output per level, and Combat Simulation adds another 3 percent ship and orbital damage per level. Their faction names reflect the active civilization—for example, the Brood evolves Endless Molting, Biomass Recursion, and Predatory Adaptation, while the Covenant executes Recursive Fabrication, Closed-Loop Reclamation, and Combat Logic Refinement. Every repeat level adds another 40 percent of the base research time and 60 percent of the base resource cost.

## Prototype acceptance criteria

- Interactive map and planet selection.
- Symmetric one-world, zero-unit starts for player and AI.
- Pre-campaign starter-faction, map-size, and enemy-difficulty selection with persistent configuration and materially different generated campaigns.
- Per-empire Human, Brood, Aegis, and Iron Covenant identities, with complete exclusive Brood and Aegis rosters for human or AI empires, including distinct economies or doctrine mechanics, research unlocks, combat stats, and presentation.
- Code-based two-to-four-player competitive lobby with separate empires, optional AI slots, a host-controlled launch, and synchronized authoritative simulation.
- Per-empire persistent fog of war that publicly identifies every empire's starting homeworld, hides unscouted forces, structures, and later expansion, preserves last-known planetary intelligence, and reveals hostile fleets only inside or inbound to friendly sensor coverage.
- Unlimited planet economy with local mine-count limits and output modifiers.
- Quantity-based construction with per-planet maxima, costs, and research gates.
- Ground and space production queues.
- Visible, selectable and groupable orbital Space Yards with independent production queues, plus quantity-based Ground Factory production speed.
- Automatic round-robin hull distribution across compatible Space Yards, with grouped multi-build as an explicit override.
- Non-instant gravity-well maneuvering, shortest-path multi-lane travel, automatic Transport embarkation, unloading, colonization, and hostile invasion.
- Expanded 780-pixel gravity wells, 86-pixel planets, separated ship formations, and empty-space deselection outside the active well.
- Four-times-larger galaxy canvas with long thin lanes, hidden scrollbars, continuous WASD camera panning, buttons-only 2%–150% map zoom, and a viewport-aware Show Whole Map control.
- Produced and docked ships receive distinct persistent orbit positions. Player phase arrivals emerge in a separated lane-facing formation that fills from the entry edge inward and hold under immediate control; AI warships advance inward and pursue orbital targets instead of remaining idle there. Loaded transports still begin a defendable landing approach that can be redirected before troops deploy.
- Oversized, scrollable 2600 × 1600 real-time ground battlefield with drag-box and click/Shift-click troop selection, right-click formation movement, automatic reload-based weapon salvos, weapon-specific animated projectile images, range rings, focus fire, functional Ground Defense turrets, and an upper-left white exit arrow.
- Automated space combat and correct recovery rules.
